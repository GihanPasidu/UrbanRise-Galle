Traffic Congestion

Colombo and other major cities face severe traffic congestion, particularly during peak hours. The average commuter spends over 2 hours daily in traffic, leading to decreased productivity, increased fuel consumption, and higher pollution levels.

Key factors contributing to this issue include:

  • Inadequate public transportation networks
  • Increasing vehicle ownership
  • Limited road infrastructure
  • Centralized business districts
Impact Level:

Waste Management

Urban areas in Sri Lanka generate approximately 1,500 metric tons of solid waste daily. Inefficient collection systems and inadequate disposal methods have led to environmental degradation, health hazards, and flooding during monsoon seasons.

Current challenges include:

  • Limited recycling infrastructure
  • Inadequate waste segregation practices
  • Improper landfill management
  • Public awareness gaps
Impact Level:

Energy Consumption

Sri Lankan cities have seen a 42% increase in energy consumption over the past five years. This surge is straining the national grid and increasing carbon emissions, as much of our energy still comes from non-renewable sources.

Contributing factors include:

  • Inefficient building designs without energy-saving features
  • Increasing use of air conditioning and appliances
  • Limited adoption of renewable energy solutions
  • Outdated infrastructure with significant energy loss
Impact Level:

Water Management

Despite abundant rainfall, urban areas frequently face water scarcity during dry seasons. Additionally, flooding during monsoons has become more severe due to poor drainage systems and reduced permeable surfaces in cities.

Key issues include:

  • Inadequate rainwater harvesting systems
  • Aging water infrastructure with high leakage rates
  • Increased concrete surfaces reducing natural drainage
  • Water pollution from industrial and domestic sources
Impact Level:
